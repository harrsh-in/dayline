# Dayline

Dayline is a simple todo and daily planning app built around tasks, habits, events, and calendar sync.

It helps users manage what they need to do, what they want to repeat, and what is already scheduled in their day.

The product is designed as a direct competitor to Todoist-style productivity apps, but with a stronger focus on minimal UX, calendar awareness, and a cleaner daily planning experience.

---

## What Dayline Is About

Most productivity apps split a person’s day into different places:

- Tasks live in a todo list.
- Events live in a calendar.
- Habits live in a separate tracker.
- Recurring work is often hard to manage clearly.

Dayline brings these pieces together in one simple experience.

The app should help users answer one question quickly:

> What does my day look like?

---

## Core Idea

Dayline combines:

- **Tasks** for work that needs to be completed.
- **Habits** for repeated personal routines.
- **Events** for scheduled commitments.
- **Calendar integrations** for real-world schedule visibility.

The app should feel lightweight, calm, and easy to use every day.

---

## Core Features

Dayline should include the following core areas.

### Tasks

Tasks are the basic unit of work.

Users should be able to create, complete, schedule, and manage tasks without friction.

Tasks may be:

- One-time.
- Scheduled.
- Recurring.
- Connected to calendar events when needed.

The task experience should be fast and minimal.

---

### Habits

Habits are repeated routines the user wants to track.

Examples:

- Drink water.
- Read for 20 minutes.
- Exercise.
- Journal.
- Review goals.

Habits should feel lighter than tasks. They should be easy to check off and should not make the app feel like a complex analytics dashboard.

---

### Events

Events represent scheduled commitments.

Examples:

- Client call.
- Doctor appointment.
- Weekly sync.
- Birthday dinner.
- Project review.

Events may be:

- One-time.
- Recurring.
- Created inside Dayline.
- Synced from Google Calendar.
- Synced from Microsoft Calendar.

---

### Recurring and One-Time Planning

Dayline should support both simple one-time planning and recurring routines.

Recurring items should be easy to understand.

The app should avoid overly complex recurrence settings unless they are necessary for normal use.

The goal is not to support every possible edge case immediately. The goal is to make common recurring behavior simple and reliable.

---

## Calendar Integration

Dayline should integrate with:

- Google Calendar.
- Microsoft Calendar.

Users should be able to connect one or multiple calendar accounts.

Calendar integration should allow users to:

- Read events from connected calendars.
- Show those events inside Dayline.
- Create Dayline events or scheduled tasks in connected calendars.
- Keep Dayline and connected calendars aligned.
- Disconnect calendars when needed.

Calendar sync should feel predictable and safe.

Users should always understand whether something belongs to Dayline, came from Google Calendar, or came from Microsoft Calendar.

---

## Multiple Calendar Accounts

Dayline should support users who have more than one calendar account.

Examples:

- Personal Google Calendar.
- Work Google Calendar.
- Microsoft 365 work calendar.
- Outlook personal calendar.

The app should let users connect multiple accounts and choose what they want to see.

The experience should remain simple even when multiple calendars are connected.

---

## Product Positioning

Dayline competes with Todoist-style apps.

The goal is not to copy another product screen by screen.

The goal is to build a cleaner and more focused daily planning app.

Dayline should compete through:

- Simpler design.
- Faster daily planning.
- Better calendar visibility.
- Tasks, habits, and events in one place.
- Fewer distractions.
- Minimal setup.
- Clear recurring behavior.
- Calm and polished UI.

---

## UX Philosophy

The UX must be extremely simple and minimal.

Dayline should avoid unnecessary complexity.

The user should not feel like they need to learn a system before they can use the app.

The experience should be obvious:

- Add what needs to be done.
- See what is planned.
- Know what is scheduled.
- Complete what is finished.
- Move what needs to be moved.

The app should feel useful within the first minute.

---

## Visual Direction

Dayline should use an appropriate color and font scheme that supports focus and clarity.

The design should feel:

- Clean.
- Calm.
- Minimal.
- Modern.
- Mature.
- Readable.
- Softly polished.

The app should not feel loud, cluttered, childish, or overly decorative.

The interface should use color carefully. Color should guide attention, not compete for it.

---

## Product Personality

Dayline should feel like a quiet planning companion.

It should not shout at the user.

It should not gamify everything.

It should not make productivity feel stressful.

It should help the user feel organized without making them spend too much time organizing.

---

## What Makes Dayline Different

Dayline is not just a todo list.

It is also not just a calendar.

It is a simple daily planning layer between tasks, habits, and scheduled events.

The key difference is the unified daily view:

> Tasks, habits, and calendar events should work together instead of living in separate apps.

---

## What Dayline Should Avoid

Dayline should avoid becoming:

- Too feature-heavy.
- Too configurable.
- Too dashboard-like.
- Too corporate.
- Too technical.
- Too visually noisy.
- Too close to a project management app.
- Too similar to existing competitors without a clear reason to exist.

If a feature does not make daily planning easier, it should be questioned.

---

## Target Users

Dayline is for people who want a simple but reliable way to manage their day.

This includes:

- Professionals.
- Students.
- Freelancers.
- Founders.
- Creators.
- Busy individuals.
- People managing both personal and work calendars.

The product should work for people who want structure but do not want a heavy productivity system.

---

## Product Goal

The goal of Dayline is simple:

> Help users bring tasks, habits, and calendar events into one clean daily planning experience.

Everything in the product should support that goal.

---

## Success Standard

Dayline succeeds if a user can open the app and immediately understand:

- What they need to do today.
- What habits are pending.
- What events are scheduled.
- What is coming next.
- What is synced from their calendar.
- What they can safely complete, move, or reschedule.

The product should reduce mental clutter, not add to it.
